Cutting Edge
Technologies – Culturally
Conscious Design

OKFocus is a forward thinking, technology driven agency based in New York City. We implement cutting edge technologies in culturally conscious ways. As children of the information age, we understand the deep impact genre defining internet products can have – creating such a thing is our paramount goal. We have worked for companies such as Google, Smirnoff and Flamingo, cultural institutions such as MOCA, New Museum and The MET and artists such as Tanlines, Nicola Formichetti and MIA – realizing our forward thinking concepts with cutting edge code. We are artists, designers and programmers with the ability to see advanced projects through each stage of development. As a young company we are optimistic for the future and driven by innovation. Client Work

  • Tanlines, Not The SameFeb.2013

    Launch Site

    Commissioned by The Creators Project, we created a interactive Alpha Channel HTML5 video experiment for True Panther Records artist, Tanlines. The site mimics Photoshop's interface – making for a culturally aware website, no manual required.

  • Metropolitan Museum of ArtJan.2013

    Launch Site

    We used advanced JavaScript techniques to create this front page animation for Metropolitan Museum of Art project, 82nd and Fifth. The animation evolves over a year as curators add new features to the site. Commissioned by our awesome friends at CHIPS–NY.

  • Flamingo, ObsessionNov.2012

    Launch Site

    We created a site to house the annual report of house leading edge trend forecasting company, Flamingo. The site allows readers to connect with Twitter and come download their bespoke selection of articles – effectively making their own unique version. The site runs on a custom built CMS.

  • David MushegainJune.2012

    Launch Site

    Website for photographer David Mushegain built on Tumblr. David told us that he likes both black and white color schemes, so we added the option for users to invert the theme at will. Using Tumblr as a CMS is a great solution for lightweight portfolio sites.

  • Nicola FormichettiMay.2012

    Launch Site

    Website utilizing fancy JavaScript and forward thinking design for Nicola Formichetti's company Two Hustlers. Nicola is Lady Gaga's stylist, fashion director of Mugler and creative director of Uniqlo. The site heavily relies on our in-house developed Plug-in, OKVideo.

  • Svpply, Tug of StoreMay.2012

    Launch Site

    Svpply is a service that lets users keep track of products they love and discover new products. They asked us to create something captivating for the launch of their API. Tug of Store is a real-time game using Websockets and Node JS where users decide if a product is cool or crap.

  • Google, Draw With Your FaceApr.2012

    Launch Site

    Commissioned by Google, we made this Hangsouts game with Aaron Meyers that allows players to draw with their face while opponents try to guess the drawing's subject. Think Pictionary or Draw Something but with more faces and funny sounds.

  • MOCA, Ends of The EarthMay.2012

    Launch Site

    For MOCA's seminal Land Art exhibit, Ends of The Earth, we created a site heavily using Google Maps API, allowing viewers to traverse the world viewing the works in the exhibition as they get background info on each work.

  • Item Idem, Concept CartFeb.2012

    Launch Site

    E-commerce site as portfolio for artist/designer Item Idem. Inspired by design patterns from online shopping, visitors are invited to make a conceptual investment by adding Idem's projects and objects to the Concept Cart, which leads to a simulated checkout process.

  • M.I.A. /\/\/\YASep.2010
